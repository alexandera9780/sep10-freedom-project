Martial Arts Gallery

Martial arts can be classified under many different things. Some of which, include things like Taekwondo, Judo, Jiu-Jitsu, and many more. The main point of all of these martial arts are ways to defend yourself. Some of them may be more hands on then others, and some might be harder to pick up compared to others, but just a few basic lessons can really help you in any need of trouble.

In martial arts, when you may not have enough money to spend on classes or you wanna go through some basic at home training regimes, there are many different things you can use. There are many softwares that you can use to help make these training regimes for you, and there are many technological advancements that have been made in this industry that are used for many different purposes.


Software Advancements

Daedo Socks

Daedo socks are socks that are used in Taekwondo, mainly in the more competitive scene. These socks are used to electronically count points during a match, however they can also be used to track where you are hitting when you are training.

Grappling Dummies

Grappling dummies are things that are usually used in more physical contact martial arts, like Jiu-Jitsu and Judo. These Grappling dummies can help with training in a way where you can practice doing certain movements without the fear of actually injuing a partner.

Muay Thai App

Some apps that can be downloaded for free on any app store can give you access to free training regimes. These apps give you just the basic examples of what to train on what day and how to do each excercise.


Hardware Advancements

Virtual Reality

One thing that people do to have immersive experiences with these martial arts is Virtual Reality. There have been many games produced on these VR systems that can help you immersify yourself with the martial art.

Advanced Motion Caption

Advanced Motion Caption is a way to see your model on a screen at the exact moment, and it can register how you do certain techniques.

Punching Bag

Some of the most well knowns ways of training is taking a good pair of gloves, and punching a punching bag. These bags can help you work on your power and speed, as well as reaction timing.
